Pan-seared duck breast

Ingredients (serves 2)

  • 2 duck breasts (skin on)
  • Kocher salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 clove of garlic, smashed
  • 1 tsp butter
  • Optional: thyme or rosemary

Method

  1. Prep the duck

    • Pat the duck breasts dry with paper towels - moisture ruins crispiness
    • Score the skin with a sharp knife, in a tight crosshatch pattern. Don't cut into the meat
    • Season with salt and pepper on both sides
    • Let it warm to room temperature before cooking (25-30 minutes)

  2. Render the fat, and sear

    • Put the meat skin side down in a cold pan. Cast iron, stainless steel and non stick are all good options.
    • Turn the heat to medium, and render the fat slowly for 10 minutes.
    • Optionally, use a meat thermometer to cook the breasts until 52 °C
    • When the skin is golden brown, get ready to flip the meat.

  3. Finish cooking

    • Turn the heat up to medium-high and flip the breasts to the skin-side
    • Add the butter to the pan, and baste the duck using a teaspoon
    • Add seasoning herbs and keep cooking for 1-2 minutes
    • Remove the duck from the pan, and let it rest for 10 minutes covered in tinfoil
